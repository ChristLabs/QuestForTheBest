The History of the Margarita

"The perfect balance of sour, sweet, salty, and boozy."

Place of Origin

The Margarita is a classic tequila-based cocktail that originated in Mexico in the 1930s or 1940s. While the true origin is debated, many believe it was first created by a bartender in Tijuana or Ensenada. This refreshing drink quickly became synonymous with warm weather, sunshine, and celebration, evolving into one of the most popular cocktails in the world.

Typical Ingredients

  • 2 oz Tequila
  • 1 oz Lime Juice (freshly squeezed)
  • 1 oz Orange Liqueur (Triple Sec or Cointreau)
  • Salt (for rimming the glass)
  • Lime Wheel (for garnish)

How to Make a Margarita

  1. Salt the rim of a chilled margarita glass by rubbing a lime wedge around it and dipping it in salt.
  2. Add tequila, lime juice, and orange liqueur to a shaker with ice.
  3. Shake until well-chilled.
  4. Strain into the prepared glass over fresh ice.
  5. Garnish with a lime wheel and serve.

Typical Glassware

The Margarita is traditionally served in a margarita glass, a special glass with a wide rim for salt. It can also be served in a rocks glass with ice for a more casual version. The wide rim allows ample space for salt, enhancing the flavor with each sip.

Influence on Other Cocktails

The Margarita has influenced a range of tequila-based drinks like the Paloma and the Tequila Sunrise. Its simple, versatile structure also led to countless variations, including flavored Margaritas made with fruits like mango, strawberry, or watermelon.
