The History of the Moscow Mule

"A refreshing kick with every sip."

Place of Origin

The Moscow Mule was invented in the early 1940s by John G. Martin and Jack Morgan in the United States. It was created as a way to promote Smirnoff Vodka, which was not very popular at the time. The cocktailâ€™s signature copper mug and its refreshing combination of ginger beer, vodka, and lime quickly gained popularity, becoming a staple in American cocktail culture.

Typical Ingredients

  • 2 oz Vodka
  • 4 oz Ginger Beer
  • 1/2 oz Lime Juice (freshly squeezed)
  • Lime Wedge (for garnish)
  • Mint Sprig (optional garnish)

How to Make a Moscow Mule

  1. Fill a copper mug with ice.
  2. Pour in the vodka and lime juice.
  3. Top with ginger beer and stir gently.
  4. Garnish with a lime wedge and mint sprig if desired.

Typical Glassware

The Moscow Mule is traditionally served in a copper mug, which helps maintain the drink's icy cold temperature. The metal mug also adds to the overall experience of drinking a refreshing Moscow Mule.

Influence on Other Cocktails

The Moscow Mule has inspired a variety of other cocktails known as "Mules," often made by substituting vodka with other spirits like bourbon (Kentucky Mule), tequila (Mexican Mule), or gin (Gin-Gin Mule). The concept of combining ginger beer with a spirit and citrus remains a popular choice for creative twists on the original.

Potential Riffs

  • Kentucky Mule: Replace vodka with bourbon for a Southern twist.
  • Mexican Mule: Use tequila instead of vodka for a bold, south-of-the-border flavor.
  • Gin-Gin Mule: Replace vodka with gin and add fresh mint for a refreshing herbal twist.
