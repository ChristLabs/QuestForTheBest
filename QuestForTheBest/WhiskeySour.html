The History of the Whiskey Sour

"A perfect balance of sweet and sour."

Place of Origin

The Whiskey Sour is a classic cocktail dating back to the 1860s. Itâ€™s believed to have originated in the United States and became popular due to its simplicity and balance of flavors. The traditional recipe combines whiskey, lemon juice, and sugar, creating a delicious balance of sweetness and tartness, perfect for sipping on any occasion.

Typical Ingredients

  • 2 oz Bourbon or Rye Whiskey
  • 3/4 oz Fresh Lemon Juice
  • 1/2 oz Simple Syrup
  • Egg White (optional)
  • Angostura Bitters (optional garnish)

How to Make a Whiskey Sour

  1. Add whiskey, lemon juice, simple syrup, and egg white (if using) into a shaker without ice.
  2. Dry shake for about 15 seconds to combine and emulsify the egg white.
  3. Add ice and shake again until well-chilled.
  4. Strain into a rocks glass over ice.
  5. Garnish with a lemon wheel and a few dashes of Angostura bitters.

Typical Glassware

The Whiskey Sour is traditionally served in a rocks glass, also known as an old-fashioned glass. This sturdy glass helps keep the cocktail cold and showcases its frothy top when egg white is used.

Influence on Other Cocktails

The Whiskey Sour has influenced many other popular cocktails, including the Boston Sour (which adds a red wine float) and the Gold Rush (which uses honey syrup instead of simple syrup). The simple template of spirit, citrus, and sugar has also been adapted for many modern cocktails.

Potential Riffs

  • Boston Sour: Add a red wine float on top of the traditional Whiskey Sour.
  • Gold Rush: Replace simple syrup with honey syrup for a richer, deeper flavor.
  • Maple Whiskey Sour: Use maple syrup instead of simple syrup for a warm, autumnal twist.
